About

Hello! My name is Jason and I compulsively pick my skin.

I’m guessing by the fact you’re reading this page you’ve heard of dermatillomania before, but maybe not! I mean, I ripped at my skin for 10 years, hours every day, and I had no idea this thing I was doing had a name. People would see my hands, and ask me if I wanted any lotion. I always knew this bad little habit wasn’t eczema, but I didn’t have the words to respond.

My feelings towards showing pictures of my hands are complicated. I’ve tried to rely on words, but I don’t think people really get it. I’ve also heard from people with BFRBs that showing pictures helps normalize them. My mind changes with the weather, but if you think seeing a picture of how my hands would help you understand me better, you can scroll down and click to see them below.

I still remember the moment when I learned the term Body Focused Repetitive Behaviour or BFRB. I was just finishing another phone call in my friend’s bathroom, and I saw, and I felt all the wind get knocked out of my chest.

Thanks for saving my life Adele. No joke.

We met up and couldn’t stop talking about it. It was dawning on me that my BFRB was damaging so much more than just my hands. Soon after, I made the decision to leave my civil engineering career to learn as much as I could about BFRBs. I was guided by earnest questions I didn’t prepare for, but really, I was preparing my entire life. My journey led me to making some amazing friends, recording some embarrassing videos, and living the life I always wanted, but never could have imagined under the weight of my BFRB.

If you’ve ever bled on an exam paper, spent your Friday night 1 inch from the mirror, or were too embarrassed to go swimming, I get it. And if you want to talk about it (or hear other people talk about it), I’m here for you [fidgetpodcast@gmail.com].

When I’m not picking my skin, or talking about picking my skin, I’m exploring the woods, or relistening to the same tiny desk concerts over and over again.
